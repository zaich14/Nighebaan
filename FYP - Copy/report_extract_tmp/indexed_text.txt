0019: Executive Summary
0020: Nigahbaan (meaning "Guardian" or "Watchful" in Urdu) is a comprehensive, web-based Smart Elderly Care and Monitoring System developed as the Final Year Project for the Spring 2026 term at the Institute of Business and Information Technology (IBIT), University of the Punjab. The platform directly addresses one of the most pressing societal challenges: ensuring the continuous dignity, safety, health, and emotional wellbeing of elderly individuals — particularly those whose families reside abroad or are unable to maintain a constant physical presence.
0021: The platform integrates five core capability pillars into a single, unified digital ecosystem:
0022: Real-time health monitoring and medication management
0023: AI-powered companionship chatbot for emotional support and cognitive engagement
0024: Comprehensive Family Dashboard for remote, real-time oversight
0025: Emergency SOS and fall detection alert system
0026: GPS-based tracking and geofencing for dementia and cognitive impairment management
0027: Nigahbaan is built on a modern, scalable technology stack: React.js for both the elderly-facing user portal and the family/caregiver dashboard, supported by a Node.js + Express.js backend API connected to a MongoDB Atlas cloud database. Third-party integrations include the OpenAI API for the NLP-powered chatbot, Firebase Cloud Messaging for push notifications, Twilio for SMS-based emergency alerts, Google Maps API for GPS and geofencing, and SendGrid for email delivery.
0028: This report documents the complete project lifecycle: from problem identification and objectives through software requirements specification, system design, technical architecture, database schema, API design, security strategy, and web application implementation.

0122: Introduction
0123: 1.1 Background & Motivation
0124: In today's fast-paced and increasingly mobile world, profound demographic shifts and urbanization trends are fundamentally reshaping family structures across Pakistan and South Asia. With more children migrating abroad for higher education or employment opportunities, and with a significant rise in elderly populations living alone, the need for intelligent, integrated elderly care solutions has never been more urgent.
0125: Elderly individuals face a complex and compounding range of challenges: the management of chronic health conditions such as hypertension, diabetes, and heart disease; the ever-present risk of falls; the cognitive and safety risks associated with Alzheimer's and dementia; and the profound psychological burden of loneliness and social isolation. The healthcare technology landscape has historically responded to these challenges in fragmented, siloed ways.
0126: Nigahbaan emerges as a direct, purposeful response to this gap. The name itself — Urdu for "Guardian" or "Watchful Eye" — reflects the platform's fundamental mission: to be a constant digital guardian for elderly individuals while simultaneously serving as a trusted bridge between them, their families, and their caregivers.
0127: 1.2 Problem Statement
0128: Despite significant advancements in healthcare and mobile technology, elderly individuals and their families continue to face serious, largely unresolved challenges:
0129: Adult children who have migrated abroad face a critical care gap — they cannot continuously monitor the health, safety, and daily wellbeing of their elderly parents.
0130: Elderly individuals suffering from dementia may wander away from home, forget medications, or be unable to seek help during a medical emergency.
0131: Loneliness and social isolation constitute a silent epidemic severely impacting the mental and emotional health of older adults.
0132: Families lack a single, integrated digital platform combining real-time health monitoring, emergency response, emotional companionship support, and caregiver coordination.
0133: Existing platforms are frequently too technically complex for elderly users with limited digital literacy, or are prohibitively expensive for middle-income Pakistani families.
0134: 1.3 Project Objectives
0135: The primary objectives of Nigahbaan, which have guided every design and development decision, are as follows:
0136: To design and develop a multi-user web platform serving three distinct user types: elderly individuals, family members, and professional caregivers — each with role-appropriate interfaces.
0137: To implement a comprehensive, real-time health monitoring module that tracks vital statistics, generates health reports, and alerts families when readings exceed safe thresholds.
0138: To build a medication reminder system with push notifications, SMS fallback, and adherence tracking.
0139: To integrate an AI-powered companionship chatbot using the OpenAI API for meaningful daily engagement and emotional support.
0140: To develop a comprehensive Family Dashboard providing remote, real-time visibility into the health vitals, GPS location, alert history, and medication adherence of linked elderly users.
0141: To implement an Emergency SOS alert system with automated fall detection dispatching multi-channel notifications instantly.
0142: To incorporate GPS tracking and geofencing functionality for managing and locating elderly individuals with dementia.
0143: To ensure accessibility for elderly users through an elderly-first UI design: large fonts, high-contrast colors, and simplified navigation.

0235: 2.2 Product Scope & Overview
0236: The Nigahbaan Elderly Care Service Platform is a standalone, cloud-hosted SaaS product. The system integrates four primary sub-systems into a unified platform:
0237: Elderly User Web Portal (React.js): A dedicated, simplified web interface for elderly individuals featuring large fonts (18pt minimum), high-contrast colors, minimal navigation steps, and a prominently displayed SOS button at all times.

0241: Out of Scope for Version 1.0: Full custom IoT sensor hardware integration, direct medical diagnosis functionality, insurance claim processing, full multi-language support beyond English/Urdu, and video consultation features (planned for Phase 3).

0244: Health Monitoring: Real-time tracking of vital health statistics with automated alerts.

0489: 3.1 Introduction
0490: A design document in the context of software development is a comprehensive, detailed layout of how a software application will look and function. It acts as a blueprint that clearly depicts what is to be built, how it operates, how it looks, and what components it consists of. The design document is critical in application development for several reasons:
0491: Clear Understanding: Ensures all stakeholders have a detailed understanding of how the software is supposed to function and how different parts interact.
0492: Guideline for Developers: Provides developers with a clear guideline to follow, reducing confusion and potential mistakes during coding.
0493: Consistency: Ensures consistency in design and implementation, especially in large teams working on different parts of the same application.
0494: Predictability: Helps predict potential issues and conflicts by requiring a thorough review of the planned application design before coding starts.
0495: Communication Tool: Allows new team members, stakeholders, or external reviewers to understand the design and function of the application quickly.
0496: Documentation: Serves as documentation for future reference or if modifications need to be made later.
0497: 3.2 System Architecture
0498: Nigahbaan follows a three-tier architecture with an additional AI/integration layer, implemented as a cloud-hosted, scalable SaaS platform. This architectural pattern provides clear separation of concerns, enabling independent development, testing, and scaling of each layer.
0499: Tier / Layer
0500: Components & Responsibilities
0501: Presentation Tier
0502: React.js Web Application — two distinct role-based interfaces: a simplified Elderly User Portal and a feature-rich Family/Caregiver Dashboard, both served as Single Page Applications (SPA). Deployed on Vercel CDN for low-latency global access.
0503: Application Tier
0504: Node.js + Express.js RESTful API server. Handles all business logic, user authentication, data validation, third-party API orchestration, notification dispatch, and real-time WebSocket communication via Socket.IO.
0505: Data Tier
0506: MongoDB Atlas cloud-hosted NoSQL database. Stores all user profiles, health records, medication schedules, GPS location history, chatbot conversation logs, emergency alert records, and audit trails. Multi-region replica sets ensure high availability.
0507: Integration Layer
0508: External services: OpenAI GPT API (chatbot), Google Maps API (GPS/geofencing), Firebase Cloud Messaging (push notifications), Twilio (SMS), SendGrid (email), and Cloudinary (PDF/media storage).

1161: Security & Deployment Architecture
1162: 4.1 Security Design
1163: 4.1.1 Authentication Architecture
1164: Nigahbaan implements stateless JWT-based authentication, which eliminates the need for server-side session storage and is inherently compatible with horizontal scaling. The authentication flow is:
1165: Registration: User password is hashed with BCrypt using a cost factor of 12 before storage. The password is never stored, logged, or transmitted in plaintext at any point.
1166: Login: BCrypt comparison is performed between the submitted password and the stored hash. On successful verification, a JWT is generated with payload containing {userId, role, issuedAt, expiresAt}. Token expiry is set to 24 hours.
1167: Refresh Tokens: A separate long-lived refresh token (7-day expiry) is issued simultaneously and stored as an HttpOnly, Secure cookie for silent session re-authentication.
1168: JWT Secret: A 256-bit cryptographically random secret stored exclusively as an environment variable — never committed to source code or version control.
1169: 4.1.2 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
1170: Role
1171: Permitted Actions
1172: Elderly User
1173: Log vitals, use chatbot, view own health data and medication schedule, trigger SOS button, update own profile settings.
1174: Family Member
1175: View all health data, GPS location, and alerts for linked elderly users. Configure geofences, medication schedules, and emergency contacts. Download health reports. Acknowledge alerts.
1176: Caregiver / Doctor
1177: View health data and medication schedules for assigned elderly users only. Add clinical notes. Cannot modify emergency contacts, geofences, or family-level configurations.
1178: System Administrator
1179: Full access to all user accounts, system configuration, audit logs. Ability to suspend, deactivate, or delete accounts. Access to platform-wide analytics.
1180: 4.1.3 Data Protection Measures
1181: Transport Security: All data in transit encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher, enforced at the load balancer / reverse proxy level.
1182: Storage Security: Sensitive fields including all health records and location history encrypted at rest using MongoDB Atlas Encryption at Rest (AES-256).
1183: API Rate Limiting: 100 requests per minute per authenticated user; 20 requests per minute for unauthenticated public endpoints. Implemented via express-rate-limit middleware.
1184: Injection Prevention: All database interactions use Mongoose parameterized queries. All incoming user input validated and sanitized using express-validator middleware.
1185: CORS Policy: Strict CORS configuration allows requests only from whitelisted frontend origins (production web application domain only).
1186: Security Headers: Helmet.js middleware adds Content-Security-Policy, X-Frame-Options, and X-XSS-Protection headers to all API responses.

1325: 6.4 Testing Strategy
1326: A multi-layered testing approach is employed to ensure the platform meets all functional requirements, performs reliably, and delivers an accessible experience for elderly users:

1328: Integration Testing: Supertest-based API endpoint testing validating correct HTTP responses, authentication enforcement, and database interactions for all major endpoints.
1329: User Acceptance Testing (UAT): Testing sessions conducted with representative users from each user class — elderly individuals, family members, and caregivers — to validate usability, accessibility, and feature completeness.
1330: End-to-End Testing: Full workflow tests covering complete user journeys such as registration → SOS alert trigger → family acknowledgement, and medication schedule creation → reminder delivery → adherence logging.
1331: Performance Testing: Load testing using Artillery.io to verify API response times remain within specified thresholds under concurrent user loads.
1332: Accessibility Testing: Manual testing with screen readers and the WAVE accessibility evaluation tool to verify WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance for the elderly user portal.

1475: Conclusion & Recommendations
1476: 8.1 Conclusion
1477: The development of Nigahbaan represents a meaningful, technically substantive, and socially impactful response to one of the most pressing societal challenges of the contemporary era: ensuring the dignity, continuous safety, and emotional wellbeing of elderly individuals in an increasingly fragmented, geographically dispersed society.
1478: By thoughtfully integrating five distinct but deeply interconnected capability pillars — real-time health monitoring with automated alerting, AI-powered conversational companionship, a comprehensive remote Family Dashboard, an emergency SOS and fall detection system, and GPS-based geofencing for dementia patient safety — Nigahbaan successfully fills the critical gap that every existing fragmented solution has individually failed to address.
1479: The platform is built on a robust, scalable, and modern technology foundation: a React.js web application with two distinct role-based interfaces — a simplified Elderly User Portal designed with elderly-first accessibility principles, and a comprehensive Family Dashboard providing real-time monitoring capabilities — powered by a Node.js + Express.js backend API and a MongoDB Atlas cloud database engineered for high availability and health data durability.
1480: The system's modular architecture ensures that each component can be independently developed, rigorously tested, and progressively deployed. The security design — encompassing JWT authentication, BCrypt password hashing, TLS encryption in transit, AES-256 encryption at rest, and comprehensive role-based access control — ensures that the platform handles sensitive health and location data with the rigor this category of information demands.
1481: Nigahbaan achieves its core project objectives: it empowers families to maintain meaningful, real-time visibility into the wellbeing of their elderly loved ones regardless of geographical distance; it provides elderly individuals with a dignified, accessible system that enhances their safety and combats their isolation; and it gives caregivers the digital tools they need to deliver higher-quality, data-informed care.
